# Monica Cognitive Engagement

## Purpose

Explore supportive, low-friction cognitive engagement ideas for Monica that go beyond word games and better match her interests, tolerance, and daily rhythms.

## Outcome Vision

A humane system that helps surface or deliver activities Monica may actually enjoy or accept, while supporting cognition, mood, orientation, reminiscence, and gentle engagement.

## Framing

This should not default to generic "brain training" apps. The design should start from Monica as a person: what feels natural, dignified, familiar, and sustainable.

## What We Know So Far

- Monica is not interested in the word games already placed on her phone.
- A better fit may involve more varied and personally meaningful forms of engagement.

## Exploration Areas

- guided reminiscence prompts
- music-linked memory prompts
- family photo and story prompts
- voice-led check-ins
- simple practical tasks with a sense of purpose
- orientation prompts tied to time, place, and routine
- light conversational or assisted exercises rather than game-like challenges

## Early Questions

- What kinds of activities does Monica naturally engage with?
- At what times of day is she most receptive?
- What formats work best: voice, visual, physical, conversational?
- What should be avoided because it feels childish, frustrating, or repetitive?
- What level of automation is welcome vs. intrusive?

## Constraints and Risks

- Must preserve dignity and avoid patronising design.
- Must fit real energy, mood, and attention patterns.
- Caregiver burden should not increase.
- Any health-sensitive framing should remain cautious and supportive rather than clinical.

## Possible Work Packages for External Agent Flows

1. Preference and context discovery framework
2. Research on non-game cognitive engagement approaches
3. Activity library design
4. Delivery channel options (voice, phone, print, HA, messaging)
5. Small pilot routine design
6. Measurement approach: what counts as working?

## Dependencies

- Better picture of Monica’s preferences and current routine
- Clarity on what devices/channels are realistically usable
- Agreement on boundaries for prompting and reminders

## Open Questions

- Is the target independent self-use, caregiver-assisted use, or both?
- Should this live inside OpenClaw, Home Assistant, or a lighter manual workflow first?
- How much personal/family content should be incorporated?

## Next Moves

- Capture Monica-specific preferences, dislikes, and energy patterns.
- Identify 3-5 candidate activity formats worth piloting.
- Decide whether to start with manual curation before any automation.
